My Teacher the Raven, an Interview with James Hart

A Micro-documentary in collaboration with The Bill Reid Gallery in downtown Vancouver. Project team of 3 over 1.5 months. Winner of best cinematography and best documentary in cohort & best sound at SFU's FCAT Film Fest 2019.

In Spring of 2019 I took on a role producing, editing, and organizing a documentary on behalf of the Bill Reid Gallery which focuses on first nations art, specifically that of the Haida people. Working with James Hart was an amazing and enriching experience. For this project I chose to take on an administrative role to challenge myself and better understand those I work under.

Producing a Well Made Object

My production roles fed into each other well; as the sound lead I took notes on important interview moments that were later used to edit the rough cut. These notes are incredibly useful to me for assessing first reactions— something an editor (who views a piece thousands of times) loses, but has to constantly maintain on behalf of the audience.

Reflection

The most important idea I will hold on to is that the “well made object” can be anything, even a documentary.
